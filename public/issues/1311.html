Bug #1311: Site lacks branding/identity

Kind bug
Product wincent.com
When Created 2009-06-13T13:57:52Z, updated 2009-06-18T06:33:11Z
Status closed
Reporter Greg Hurrell
Tags no tags

Description

A visitor landing on a post or a wiki article may ask "who is behind this site?", and they really won't have a clue at all. This is because there are no "by lines" or other identifying information on those items.

The only branding on the new part of the site is a tiny monochromatic logo in the top-right next to the site name. The site name itself, wincent.com, doesn't really mean much.

Pages belonging to the old parts of the site probably had more branding:

  • The old blog: http://wincent.com/a/about/wincent/weblog/
  • Product listing: http://wincent.com/a/products/
  • etc

By the time all of the old parts of the site have been migrated over, I think a fair bit of work needs to be done on branding and identity. That really means two things:

  1. A visitor landing on any given page should be able to answer the questions, "Who made this page?"
  2. All pages on the site should have a distinctive look and feel; the current black-and-white scheme is certainly subdued and professional, but it's soulless
