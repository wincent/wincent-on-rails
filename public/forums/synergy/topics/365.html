  2. Greg Hurrell 2008-05-28T18:07:03Z

    One thing I wish it would do is use the iTunes artwork, when available (which in my case is 90% of the time). Even though I have the artwork in iTunes, Synergy displays wrong artwork it must have found on the web.

    You can get rid of the downloaded art from ~/Library/Application Support/Synergy/Album Covers folder and Synergy will use the art from iTunes. The issue that you've discovered is that Synergy prioritizes downloaded art, so if you don't have art and Synergy downloads the wrong art, then adding the correct art to the track won't help because Synergy will continue to use the downloaded art.

    This prioritization was originally chosen for performance reasons — ticket #54 is for tracking the issue — but given today's machines are more powerful there is little reason to worry about this kind of micro-optimization any more.

    My menu bar buttons change every time I restart the computer, and the only way I've found to change them (from offensive or ugly ones) is to change the button spacing and click Apply. So now, every time I restart, I have to open up Synergy and do this little dance until a set of buttons comes up that I can live with. The button style pop-up menu is disabled.

    Go into the "Advanced" tab of the preferences and turn off the "random" button style rotation.
