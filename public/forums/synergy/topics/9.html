Greg Hurrell
2004-01-31T08:05:38Z


    I think that most people who make predictions about when things will be done in the future find their predictions coming back to bite them, and I am definitely no exception!
    

    Right now the Synergy code base is over 40,000 lines of code (which includes a fair bit of in-code documentation) but it's a sizeable project. One of the reasons that the album downloads issue is taking so long to fix is that I am finding that I have to make widespread changes throughout the codebase. Also, the code is fairly strongly wedded to Amazon.com, which means it's not easy to just switch it over to another source. In general I am finding it slow to make changes to Synergy because it has grown far beyond its original purpose. With a bit more foresight I could've saved myself a lot of grief...
    

    Synergy Advance will also have album cover management features, but they will be modular in nature and won't be tied to just Amazon. Of course, this doesn't help the current Synergy situation, which really is a separate issue.
    

    I apologise for the slow progress, but I am working on it. My personal circumstances have changed somewhat of late (moving from one country to another) and that also has affected my development resources, but now I am basically full-time working on this stuff (at least until my savings run out!).
    

    My time is currently divided into three parts: firstly, maintaining customer relations (responding to emails, reading feedback, processing payments, admin stuff, website maintenance); secondly, working on updates to existing Wincent products; and thirdly, working on future Wincent products.
    

    In terms of the latter, I'm 95% done working on a replacement for Apple's Fast User Switching menu that doesn't take up so much screen real-estate, and the rest of my time goes into developing a framework called WOBase which is at heart not only of Synergy Advance, but GetSmart Pro and just about every other software project I have in mind for the future. The framework is up and running, functional and already useful (in fact, it's in my WinHex application which has already been released to the public). I'm not going to make any predictions about when the other apps will be ready for release though, although I can say that it is my intention to release early and often in the beginning stages (ie. at version 0.1) so that you can get lots of snapshots of how things are progressing.
